As you might have noticed, I haven't been keeping up with posting new content to the blog.

The act of turning the ideas, problems, and solutions into a readable blog post is something I procrastinate a lot.
With the recent advancements in AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) in particular, I have been experimenting with how they can help me ship a blog post faster.

I do not want to this blog to become an AI slopfest, but I do recognize that they can be very useful in turning ideas, code, and solutions into readable posts pretty quickly.
I am not going to 'yolo' LLM output onto the blog and see what sticks.
Everything the LLM outputs will still be reviewed, and potentially edited and altered to fit.

When reading something I also want to know if it is an AI article I am reading, or that it is written by a human.
Therefore, when a post is written with AI **assistance**, I will put a disclosure in the footer linking to this blog post.

Bottom line:
I will still be reviewing the AI output, and ensure that the ideas, technical content, and code in the post are accurate.
The AI assistance will mostly be with writing text, structuring sentences, and organizing the content.
